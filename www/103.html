SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

103 – BRADYCARDIA

Assessment

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition. If the patient will not tolerate a NRB, apply Oxygen at 6 Lpm BNC.
  2. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Glucose check

AEMT

  1. INT or IV NS TKO
  2. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly until normal levels achieved. Try to avoid large swings in serum glucose levels.

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit
  2. If patient is asymptomatic and heart rate is less than 60 beats per minute, transport and observe
  3. If PVCs are present with bradycardia do NOT administer Lidocaine.
  4. Adults –
    1. If systolic BP <90 mmHg and heart rate <60/min
    2. Administer Atropine 1 mg up to 0.04 mg/kg (3 mg for adults) (No peds dosing)
    3. If 2nd and 3rd degree blocks are present apply transcutaneous pacer pads (if available)
    4. If systolic BP <90 mmHg and heart rate <60/min continue
    5. Administer epinephrine at 2–10 mcg over 1 min – may be repeated in 5 min
    6. If systolic BP <90 mmHg and heart rate <60/min continues
    7. Notify Medical Control and begin cardiac pacing per protocol
    8. Consider Dopamine 2–20 mcg/kg/min as a continuous IV infusion to increase heart rate.
  5. Pediatric –
    1. Heart rates <60/min for infant or child
    2. Signs of poor perfusion, respiratory distress, or hypotension? Yes – Start chest compressions, IV/IO
    3. Epinephrine 1:10,000 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO q 4 min
    4. Contact Medical Control
    5. Consider external cardiac pacing
    6. Consider Dopamine 2–20 mcg/kg/min as a continuous IV infusion to increase heart rate
