SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

104 – ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME / STEMI

Assessment

  • Quality, duration and radiation of pain
  • Substernal oppressive chest pain (crushing or squeezing)
  • Nausea and/or vomiting
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cool, clammy skin
  • Palpitations
  • Anxiety or restlessness
  • Abnormal pulse rate or rhythm
  • History of Coronary Artery Disease or AMI
  • Currently taking cardiac medications
  • JVD
  • Distal pulse for equality/strength to assess for aneurysm
  • Diaphoresis, pallor, cyanosis
  • Breath sounds – congestion, rales, wheezing
  • Motor deficits

PQRST Cardiac Assessment Mnemonic

  • P: Placement of pain/discomfort (triggers or aggravators)
  • Q: Quality of pain
  • R: Radiation of pain
  • S: Severity of pain (scale of 1–10)
  • T: Time of onset and progression

Note: Elderly, female, and diabetic patients may have atypical symptoms (nausea, fatigue, SOB). Screen for silent MI in these populations.

EMR / EMT

  1. Oxygen at 2–6 Lpm BNC; airway maintenance as needed
  2. If severe respiratory distress: Oxygen 100% via NRB at 12–15 Lpm
  3. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry; maintain SpO₂ > 94%
  2. 324 mg chewable aspirin if not contraindicated or already taken
  3. Assist with patient’s NTG SL if prescribed
  4. Glucose check
  5. Cardiac monitor; obtain 12-lead and transmit if possible

AEMT

  1. INT or IV NS TKO
  2. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly
  3. If systolic BP >110 and symptomatic, assist with 1 NTG tab or spray
  4. Reassess every 5 minutes; max 3 doses (including any taken prior)
  5. Contact Medical Control for further dosing
  6. Note: Use caution with ED medication history

Paramedic

  1. Transport AMI-positive patients to appropriate cardiac facility
  2. Transmit EKG within 10 min
  3. Aspirin 324 mg PO if not taken in last 24 hrs
  4. Nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL (if BP >100 mmHg) and 1" NTG paste
  5. If systolic BP <100 mmHg, give 250 ml NS bolus
  6. If PVCs >15/min: Lidocaine 1–1.5 mg/kg, repeat to max 3 mg/kg
  7. Continue nitrate therapy and complete thrombolytic screening
  8. Treat pain >7 per pain protocol
  9. If STEMI identified, follow SCEMS STEMI protocol

Sullivan County EMS STEMI Protocol

  1. Obtain history, vitals, and cardiac rhythm
  2. 12-lead EKG and transmit to hospital
  3. Contact Medical Control if STEMI suspected
  4. Break STEMI kit seal but retain seal materials
  5. Aspirin 324 mg PO chewed
  6. IV NS KVO; draw blood before infusion
  7. NTG 0.4 mg SL every 5 min if BP >100 mmHg; consider paste
  8. Heparin 5000 U IVP + Brilinta 180 mg PO if STEMI confirmed
  9. If BP <100 mmHg, consider NS bolus
  10. Contact Medical Control
  11. Morphine 2–10 mg IV slowly
  12. Zofran 4 mg IV/IM for nausea
  13. Monitor VS & EKG; update receiving facility; complete thrombolytic screen
