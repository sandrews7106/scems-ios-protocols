SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

105 – CHEST PAIN / NON-CARDIAC

Assessment

  • Determine quality, duration, and radiation of pain – atypical chest pain
  • No nausea or vomiting, no shortness of breath
  • No cool, clammy skin
  • History of chest injury or persistent cough
  • No history of CAD or AMI; not currently taking cardiac medications
  • Distal pulse assessment for aneurysm risk
  • No diaphoresis, pallor, or cyanosis
  • Normal breath sounds
  • PQRST evaluation of pain

Note: Elderly, female, and diabetic patients may present with vague symptoms such as nausea, weakness, or shortness of breath. Screen for possible silent MI.

EMR

  1. Oxygen 2–6 Lpm via BNC or 12–15 Lpm NRB if in respiratory distress
  2. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Administer 325 mg chewable aspirin if not already taken within 24 hours
  3. Blood glucose check

AEMT

  1. IV/INT Normal Saline TKO
  2. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN to normalize levels
  3. If SBP >110 and symptomatic, assist with 1 SL nitroglycerin every 5 minutes, max 3 doses
  4. Contact Medical Control for additional nitroglycerin if needed
  5. Use caution with recent erectile dysfunction medication use — may cause profound hypotension

Paramedic

  1. Obtain and transmit 12-lead EKG if available
  2. If SBP ≥100, administer nitroglycerin SL or spray
  3. If chest pain >7/10, follow pain management protocol
  4. Contact Medical Control
  5. Transport

Note

  • Cardiac disease may present with atypical symptoms — err on the side of caution
