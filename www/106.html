SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

106 – PULSELESS ELECTRICAL ACTIVITY (PEA)

Assessment

  • No palpable pulse despite organized cardiac rhythm on monitor
  • Signs of poor perfusion: unresponsiveness, apnea, cyanosis
  • Possible reversible causes: H’s and T’s (e.g., Hypovolemia, Tamponade, Tension Pneumothorax, Thrombosis, etc.)

EMR / EMT

  1. Begin CPR immediately
  2. Apply AED and follow prompts
  3. Administer 100% oxygen via BVM
  4. Use airway adjuncts as needed (OPA/NPA)

AEMT

  1. Establish IV/IO access
  2. Administer Normal Saline bolus 20 cc/kg

Paramedic

  1. Continue high-quality CPR
  2. Administer Epinephrine 1:10,000 – 1 mg IV/IO every 3–5 minutes
  3. Identify and treat reversible causes (H’s and T’s)
  4. Consider advanced airway placement as needed

Notes

  • Ensure high-quality compressions and minimal interruptions
  • Reassess frequently for ROSC (Return of Spontaneous Circulation)
  • Communicate clearly with receiving facility during resuscitation
