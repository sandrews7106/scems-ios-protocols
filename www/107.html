SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

107 – PREMATURE VENTRICULAR CONTRACTIONS (PVC)

Assessment

  • Any PVC in acute MI setting with associated chest pain
  • Multifocal PVCs
  • Unifocal PVCs >15/min
  • Salvos, couplets, or runs of V-Tach (three or more PVCs in a row) with symptoms
  • PVCs occurring on or near the T-wave (“R-on-T” phenomenon)

EMR

  1. Oxygen 100% and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Glucose check

AEMT

  1. INT or IV NS TKO
  2. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly until normal levels achieved
  3. Avoid large swings in serum glucose levels

Paramedic

  1. EKG monitoring with 12-lead; transmit if available
  2. If PVCs are present with heart rate >60/min:
    1. Lidocaine 1.5 mg/kg IV over 1 minute (Peds: 1.0 mg/kg), repeat up to 3 mg/kg
    2. If PVCs are abolished, initiate Lidocaine drip at 2–4 mg/min
    3. Use half-dose for subsequent Lidocaine doses in patients >70 y/o or with hepatic disease
    4. If no response to Lidocaine, consider Amiodarone 150–300 mg over 10 minutes IV/IO
