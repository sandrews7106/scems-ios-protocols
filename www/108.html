SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

108 – NARROW COMPLEX TACHYCARDIA

Assessment

  • Paroxysmal Atrial Tachycardia
  • Atrial Flutter
  • Atrial Fibrillation
  • Symptomatic patient with:
    • Dyspnea
    • Chest pain
    • Radiating pain
    • Altered mental status
    • Hypotension (systolic BP <90 mmHg)
    • Diaphoresis

EMR / EMT

  1. Administer oxygen to maintain SpO₂ ≥ 94%
  2. Glucose check (EMT skill)

AEMT

  1. Establish IV with Normal Saline (INT not acceptable)

Paramedic

  1. Determine if the patient is stable or unstable
  2. If unstable or becomes unstable at any point → proceed to synchronized cardioversion
  3. If stable:
    1. Obtain 12-lead EKG; transmit if capable
    2. Determine if the rhythm is regular or irregular
    3. If rhythm is regular (or unknown), attempt vagal maneuvers
    4. Administer Adenosine 12 mg IV push; may consider starting with 6 mg in elderly/small patients
    5. Repeat 12 mg if no effect
  4. If unstable and cardioversion is required:
    1. Consider sedation per Sedation Protocol
    2. Cardiovert at 100 joules (first attempt)
    3. Pediatrics: 0.5 J/kg then 1 J/kg
    4. Second attempt: 200 joules if needed
    5. Contact Medical Control if unable to convert
