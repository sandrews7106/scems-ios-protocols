SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

110 – VENTRICULAR ASYSTOLE

Assessment

  • No pulse or respirations
  • Confirm cardiac rhythm with electrodes
  • Record in two leads to confirm asystole and rule out fine V-Fib

EMR / EMT

  1. Apply AED
  2. Begin CPR appropriate to patient age
  3. Administer 100% oxygen and maintain airway as appropriate

AEMT

  1. Check blood glucose
  2. Administer IV NS bolus (20 cc/kg)
  3. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly until normal levels achieved

Paramedic

  1. Administer Epinephrine 1:10,000 1 mg IV/IO every 3–5 minutes
    Pediatrics: 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO q 3–5 min
  2. Consider Sodium Bicarbonate 1 mEq/kg IV/IO, then 0.5 mEq/kg every 10 minutes
  3. Consider Magnesium Sulfate 1–2 g slow IV push over 2 minutes
    (No pediatric dose)
  4. Defibrillate if fine V-Fib is suspected
  5. Consider external pacing if witnessed arrest and asystole is initial rhythm
  6. Discontinue efforts if criteria are met per Discontinuation / Withholding of Life Support SOP

Reversible Causes

  • Hypovolemia
  • Hypoxia
  • Hydrogen ion (acidosis)
  • Hyperkalemia / Hypokalemia
  • Hypothermia
  • Tablets / Drug overdose
  • Tamponade (cardiac)
  • Tension pneumothorax
  • Thrombosis – Heart (MI)
  • Thrombosis – Lungs (PE)
