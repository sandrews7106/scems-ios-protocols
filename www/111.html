SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

111 – VENTRICULAR FIBRILLATION / PULSELESS VENTRICULAR TACHYCARDIA

Assessment

  • Ventricular Fibrillation (VF) or Pulseless Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)
  • Pulseless and apneic
  • Confirm and record cardiac rhythm in two leads

EMR / EMT

  1. Apply AED
  2. Begin CPR appropriate for patient’s age
  3. Administer 100% oxygen and maintain airway as appropriate

AEMT

  1. Establish IV with Normal Saline TKO

Paramedic

  1. Administer Epinephrine 1:10,000 – 1 mg IV/IO every 4 minutes
    (Peds: 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO q 4 min)
  2. Defibrillate per manufacturer’s recommendation
  3. Perform 2 minutes of CPR immediately after each shock before rhythm check
  4. If rhythm unchanged, repeat defibrillation and CPR cycles
    (Peds: begin defibrillation at 2 J/kg)
  5. Amiodarone 300 mg IV/IO bolus; may repeat at 150 mg after 5 minutes
    (Peds: 5 mg/kg)
  6. For prolonged arrest or acidosis:
    1. Sodium Bicarbonate 1 mEq/kg IV/IO; repeat at 0.5 mEq/kg every 10 minutes
  7. Consider Magnesium Sulfate 1–2 g IV push over 2 minutes
    (No pediatric dosage)

Notes

  • Defibrillation should not be delayed for any reason other than safety
  • Prompt defibrillation is critical to survival
  • After two drug cycles, airway management, and IV/IO access — consider transport
