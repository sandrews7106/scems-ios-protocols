SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

112 – VENTRICULAR TACHYCARDIA WITH A PULSE

Assessment

  • Confirm and record cardiac rhythm in two leads
  • Check for palpable carotid pulse
  • Altered mental status
  • Dyspnea
  • Chest pain or discomfort, suspected AMI
  • Hypotension (SBP <90 mmHg)
  • CHF or pulmonary edema
  • HR >150/min with QRS >0.12 sec (≥3 small blocks)

EMR

  1. Administer 100% oxygen and maintain airway as needed
  2. Provide supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Blood glucose check

AEMT

  1. IV/INT with Normal Saline TKO
  2. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly until glucose levels normalize

Paramedic

  1. 12-lead EKG and transmit
  2. If stable, regular, and monomorphic: administer Adenosine 12 mg rapid IV push
    (Consider 6 mg in smaller/elderly patients)
  3. If rhythm appears to be Torsades de Pointes, refer to that protocol
  4. If SBP <90 mmHg, prepare for synchronized cardioversion:
    1. Sedate as needed per sedation protocol
    2. Cardiovert beginning at manufacturer’s recommended energy level
      (Peds: start at 0.5 J/kg)
  5. If conversion occurs, monitor closely and transport
  6. If no conversion: Amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 min
    (Peds: 5 mg/kg IV)
  7. If SBP >90 mmHg and stable:
    1. Attempt Valsalva maneuver for 10 seconds
    2. Administer Amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 min
      (Peds: 5 mg/kg)
    3. Repeat dosing up to 3 times if no conversion
      (Peds: 3 doses of 5 mg/kg)
  8. Contact Medical Control as needed
