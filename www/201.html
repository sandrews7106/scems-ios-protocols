SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

201 – CHEMICAL EXPOSURE

Special Note

  • Personnel safety is the highest priority.
  • Do not handle the patient unless they have been decontaminated.
  • All EMS treatment should occur in the Support Zone after decontamination.
  • Appropriate PPE will be utilized.

Assessment

  • History of exposure to chemical
  • Identify substance and verify with documentation if possible (MSDS if available)
  • Stay within the appropriate zone for protection

All Providers (EMR, EMT, AEMT, Paramedic)

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. Supportive care
  3. IV NS TKO or INT PRN

Treatment – Standing Orders

If Internal Exposure and Conscious:

  1. Treat as Drug Ingestion
  2. Contact Medical Control

If External Exposure:

  1. Remove clothing, jewelry, glasses, and contacts
  2. Decontaminate with appropriate PPE
  3. Powder or like substance:
    1. Brush off patient
    2. Flush with copious amounts of water for at least 20 minutes
    3. Assess for hypothermia every 5 minutes
    4. Continue flushing during transport if necessary
  4. Liquid substance:
    1. Flush with copious amounts of water for at least 20 minutes
    2. Assess for hypothermia every 5 minutes
    3. Continue flushing during transport if necessary

If Inhalation:

  1. Consider SCBA use
  2. Remove victim from source ensuring no danger to EMS personnel
  3. Administer 100% oxygen and maintain airway

If Ocular:

  1. Flush eye with tap water or normal saline for 15 minutes
  2. Contact Medical Control

Note

  • When appropriate, consult with HazMat prior to transport to ensure proper treatment and decontamination
