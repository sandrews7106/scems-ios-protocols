SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

202 – DRUG INGESTION

Assessment

  • History of drug ingestion
  • Level of consciousness (Alert, Verbal, Pain, or Unresponsive)
  • Neurological status (LOC, pupils)
  • General appearance (sweating, dry or flushed skin, signs of trauma)

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. Ensure personnel protection from toxin and/or unruly patient
  3. Utilize Narcan autoinjector (or pre-filled system per manufacturer)
  4. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Glucose check

AEMT

  1. IV NS TKO or INT PRN
  2. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly (peds: 2 cc/kg D25 IV)
  3. If no IV/IO and gag reflex intact, 1 tube of instant glucose
  4. Narcan 0.4–2 mg IV/IO/IM/IN titrated to adequate ventilation (peds 0.1 mg/kg IVP/IN)
  5. Repeat Narcan as needed; higher doses for synthetic opiates
  6. Physically manage airway if no response after 8 mg Narcan

Paramedic

  1. EKG monitor and 12-lead EKG
  2. For seizures: Ativan 1–2 mg IV (peds 0.1 mg/kg q1 min to 1 mg max)
  3. OR Valium 2–5 mg IV (peds 0.2 mg/kg)
  4. OR Versed 2–5 mg IVP (peds 0.1 mg/kg)

Notes

  • Poison Control may be contacted for information only
  • Treatment should follow protocols unless otherwise directed by Medical Control
