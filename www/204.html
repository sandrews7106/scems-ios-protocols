SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

204 – HYPERTHERMIA

Assessment

  • History of exposure to warm temperature
  • Usually seen with increased exertion
  • Febrile
  • Hot and dry or warm and moist skin
  • May be hypotensive
  • History of therapeutic drug use (e.g., antipsychotics), substance abuse
  • Poor skin turgor
  • Signs of hypovolemic shock
  • Dark urine (suggestive of muscle breakdown/kidney damage)
  • Tachycardia, hyperventilation, hypertension
  • Neurologic changes: lightheadedness, confusion to coma, seizures

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to the patient’s condition
  2. Remove clothing, apply wet linens or pads to groin and axillae
  3. Expose to circulating air
  4. Move patient to a cool environment (shade, A/C)
  5. Do not cool to point of shivering

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Glucose check

AEMT

  1. IV NS or LR 20 cc/kg bolus (peds 20 cc/kg)
  2. Repeat fluid bolus if needed
  3. Oral rehydration if airway intact
  4. Gently massage extremities to prevent cold-induced vasoconstriction
  5. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN to normalize glucose levels

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit

Notes

  1. Time is critical to decrease body temperature
  2. Do not use iced IV saline; slightly cooled fluids are acceptable
  3. Hyperthermia causes may include:
    1. Antipsychotics (Thorazine®, Haldol®)
    2. Antidepressants (Elavil®, Norpramin®, Tofranil®)
    3. Amphetamines
    4. MAO inhibitors (Nardil®, Marplan®)
    5. Anticholinergics (Atropine, Cogentin, Scopolamine)
    6. Illicit drugs (Cocaine, PCP, LSD, Ecstasy)
