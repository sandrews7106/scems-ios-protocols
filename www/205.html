SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

205 – HYPOTHERMIA

Assessment

  • History of exposure to cold temperature including duration
  • Core body temperature <92°F
  • Drug/Alcohol use
  • CNS depressants
  • Examine for associated trauma
  • Immersion in cold water
  • Predisposing medical condition
  • Vital signs: bradycardia, hypotension, cold extremities
  • Neurologic: confusion, altered LOC, coma

EMR / EMT

  1. Oxygen 100% at 12–15 LPM via NRB
  2. Remove patient from cold environment
  3. Remove wet clothing and cover with warm, dry blankets
  4. Evaluate pulse for one full minute (do not perform CPR unless no pulse)
  5. Handle patient gently (aggressive movement may trigger V-Fib)
  6. Do not allow patient to walk or exert themselves
  7. Do not massage extremities
  8. Glucose check (EMT skill)

AEMT

  1. IV NS 75 cc/hr, warmed if possible (peds 4 cc/kg/hr, max 150 cc/hr)
  2. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly until normal glucose levels achieved
  3. If unresponsive: Narcan 0.4 mg IV/IO/IM/IN (peds 0.1 mg/kg slow IVP/IN)

Paramedic

  1. EKG Monitor – no CPR if bradycardic rhythm exists
  2. If body temperature >85°F – follow normal arrest protocols
  3. If <85°F and patient in V-Fib:
    1. Defibrillate @ 100j, increase as needed to 200j max (120j, 150j, 200j)
    2. Peds: 2 j/kg then 4 j/kg
    3. Withhold medications until warmed to >85°F
    4. Continue CPR and active rewarming

Notes

  • If alert and responsive: rewarm passively with:
    1. Heat packs or warm water bottles to groin, axillae, and neck
  • If unresponsive: rewarm actively with:
    1. Gradual increase in room temp, cover with blankets
  • Signs/symptoms by temperature:
    1. 95°F – amnesia, poor judgment, hyperventilation, bradycardia, shivering
    2. 90°F – loss of coordination, shallow breathing, bradycardia, shivering ceases
    3. 85°F – decreased LOC, slow respirations, a-fib, hypotension, ventricular irritability
