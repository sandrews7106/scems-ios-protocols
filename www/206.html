SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

206 – NEAR DROWNING

Assessment

  • History compatible with near drowning
  • Suspect hypothermia in “cold water” near drowning
  • Suspect cervical spine injury

EMR / EMT

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. The Heimlich Maneuver may be indicated for airway obstruction
  3. Gastric decompression may be necessary to ensure adequate respirations or ventilations
  4. If necessary, ventilations may be started prior to patient’s removal from water
  5. Remove patient from water, clear airway while protecting the C-spine ASAP
  6. If patient is unconscious and pulseless – refer to the Cardiac Arrest Protocol
  7. If hypothermic – go to hypothermia protocol
  8. Supportive care

AEMT

  1. INT or IV NS TKO
  2. If hypotensive, give 20 cc/kg bolus of fluid (peds: 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. EKG Monitor and treatment specific for the arrhythmia
  2. Reinforce the need to transport and evaluate all patients with a submersion incident
  3. Consider C-Spine protection
