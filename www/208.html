SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

208 – POISONOUS SNAKE BITE

Assessment

  • Protect yourself from exposure—snakes can envenomate up to one hour after death.
  • Determine type of snake, time of bite, and changes in symptoms since occurrence.
  • If possible, transport the dead snake in a secure container with the patient.
  • Paresthesia (numbness or tingling of mouth, tongue, or other areas)
  • Local pain
  • Metallic or unusual taste
  • Chills, nausea, vomiting, headache, dysphagia
  • Hypotension
  • Fever
  • Local edema, blebs (blisters), discoloration, bite wound configuration

EMR

  1. Remove rings and bracelets from the patient
  2. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  3. Immobilize affected area with extremity in a neutral position
  4. Mark swelling progression at initial assessment and every 5 minutes
  5. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. INT or IV NS TKO
  2. If hypotensive: NS bolus 20 cc/kg (peds 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12-lead EKG, transmit if capable
  2. Consider Ativan, Valium, or Versed for extreme anxiety – contact Medical Control prior to use
  3. DO NOT USE: ice, tourniquets, clamps, or constricting bands near the bite site
  4. Remove any such items if already applied
  5. DO NOT place IV in affected extremity if possible
