SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

210 – CARBON MONOXIDE EXPOSURE

Assessment

  • Known or suspected CO exposure (e.g., active fire scene)
  • Suspected source/duration of exposure
  • Known or possible pregnancy
  • Measured atmospheric levels
  • Past medical history, medications
  • Altered mental status, dizziness
  • Headache, nausea, vomiting
  • Chest pain, respiratory distress
  • Neurological impairments
  • Vision problems, reddened eyes
  • Tachycardia, tachypnea
  • Arrhythmias, seizures, coma

All Levels

  1. Measure Carbon Monoxide COHb % (SpCO)
  2. If SpCO is 0–5%: no further SpCO evaluation required*
  3. If SpCO <15% and SpO₂ >90% with no symptoms: no treatment required*
  4. If SpCO <15% and SpO₂ >90% with symptoms: transport to ED
  5. If SpCO >15%: oxygen via NRB and transport to ED
  6. For cardiac/respiratory/neurologic symptoms: follow appropriate protocol

Notes

  • Use Scene Rehabilitation Protocol when monitoring fire scene responders
  • *Pregnant patients should be advised SpCO levels reflect adult, fetal levels may be higher
  • Recommend evaluation of home/work environment and smoking cessation if applicable
  • Low COHb does not rule out other fire-related toxins
  • Consider HazMat Cyanide protocol in obtunded fire victims
  • Evaluate other possible causes when CO exposure is suspected
