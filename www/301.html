SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

301 – ABDOMINAL PAIN

Assessment

  • Assess Airway, Breathing, and Circulation
  • Onset and location of pain
  • Pain duration, nature (sharp, dull, cramping), and radiation
  • Presence of nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation
  • Last oral intake and bowel movement
  • Signs of shock: cool, clammy skin; tachycardia; hypotension
  • Assess for vaginal bleeding if applicable

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance as needed
  2. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Monitor vital signs and reassess frequently
  3. Consider position of comfort

AEMT

  1. IV NS TKO or INT
  2. If hypotensive, administer 20 cc/kg NS bolus (peds: 10–20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. Consider pain management protocol if needed
  3. Contact Medical Control for further guidance
