SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

302 – ACUTE PULMONARY EDEMA / CHF

Assessment

  • Focus assessment of Airway, Breathing, and Circulation
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cyanosis
  • Pedal Edema
  • Profuse sweating, or cool and clammy skin
  • Erect posture
  • Distended neck veins (engorged, pulsating) – late sign
  • Bilateral rales/wheezes
  • Tachycardia (pulse >100 bpm)
  • History of CHF or other heart disease, or renal dialysis
  • Lasix or Digoxin on medication list

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. If respirations <10/min or >30/min, assist with BVM and 100% O2
  3. Keep patient in upright seated position

EMT

  1. If the patient has Albuterol prescribed, assist with one treatment
  2. May assist with patient’s Nitroglycerin SL

AEMT

  1. IV/INT
  2. If SBP >110 and symptomatic, assist patient with 1 NTG dose SL; reassess q 5 min, max 3 doses. Use caution with ED meds
  3. If SBP >90 mmHg:
    1. Assess for crackles, wheezes, JVD, edema, cyanosis, diaphoresis, RR >25 or <10
    2. 1 NTG 0.4 mg SL + 1” NTG paste to chest wall. Repeat NTG q 5 min. Discontinue if SBP <100
    3. Albuterol 2.5 mg/3 cc NS neb q 5 min, max 3 doses
  4. If SBP <90 mmHg: oxygen, rapid transport, hypotension protocol, contact Medical Control
  5. Consider CPAP if patient in severe distress and ALS unavailable

Paramedic

  1. CPAP if severe distress and no contraindications
  2. Treatment – Protocol:
    1. Dopamine 400 mg/250 cc D5W IV @ 15 cc/hr, titrate if hypotensive and symptomatic (SBP <90)
    2. OR Epinephrine 2–10 mcg over 1 min – may repeat in 5 min
