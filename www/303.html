SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

303 – ALLERGIC REACTION / ANAPHYLAXIS

Assessment

  • Exposure to allergen
  • Respiratory distress
  • Swelling of face, lips, tongue, or airway
  • Hives, rash, itching
  • Hypotension or signs of shock
  • Wheezing or stridor
  • Abdominal pain or vomiting (common in peds)

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. If available, assist patient with prescribed Epinephrine Auto-Injector

EMT

  1. Assist with Epinephrine Auto-Injector if prescribed and patient meets criteria
  2. Assist with prescribed Albuterol inhaler if wheezing is present

AEMT

  1. IV NS TKO or INT
  2. If SBP <90 or severe symptoms:
    1. Epinephrine 1:1,000 0.3–0.5 mg IM (peds: 0.01 mg/kg IM, max 0.3 mg)
    2. Benadryl 25–50 mg IM or slow IV (peds: 1 mg/kg)
    3. Albuterol 2.5 mg/3 mL NS neb, may repeat ×2 if wheezing persists
    4. Consider Epinephrine 1:10,000 IV/IO 0.1 mg slow push if shock persists

Paramedic

  1. 12-lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. Steroids: Solumedrol 125 mg IV (peds: contact Medical Control)
  3. Push-Dose Epinephrine 2–10 mcg IV/IO every 1–2 minutes if SBP remains <90 mmHg after fluids
  4. Consider CPAP if in severe respiratory distress and no contraindications

Notes

  • Always monitor airway status closely; early airway compromise can be subtle
  • Transport is mandatory after Epinephrine administration
  • Pediatric doses should be carefully weight-based
