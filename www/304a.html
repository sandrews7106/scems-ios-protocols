SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

304A – CINCINNATI PREHOSPITAL STROKE SCALE

Primary Evaluation

  • Facial Droop: Ask the patient to show teeth or smile.
    • Normal: Both sides of face move equally.
    • Abnormal: One side does not move as well as the other.
  • Arm Drift: Patient closes eyes and holds both arms out.
    • Normal: Arms move equally or do not move at all.
    • Abnormal: One arm drifts down or does not move.
  • Speech: Ask patient to say, “You can’t teach an old dog new tricks.”
    • Normal: Correct words with no slurring.
    • Abnormal: Slurred words, wrong words, or inability to speak.

Additional Note

  • Use for evaluation of acute, non-comatose, non-traumatic neurologic complaints.

Detailed Steps

  • Facial/Smile or Grimace: Ask patient to show teeth or smile.
    • Normal: Both sides move equally.
    • Abnormal: One side does not move well.
  • Arm Drift: Ask patient to close eyes and hold arms straight for 10 seconds.
    • Normal: Arms move equally or do not move.
    • Abnormal: One arm drifts or does not move.
  • Speech: Ask patient to say, “It is sunny outside today.”
    • Normal: Words spoken clearly and correctly.
    • Abnormal: Slurred, incorrect, or no speech.
