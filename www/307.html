SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

307 – HYPERGLYCEMIA ASSOCIATED WITH DIABETES

Assessment

  • History of onset
  • Altered level of consciousness
  • Pulse: tachycardia, thready pulse
  • Respirations (Kussmaul-Kien – air hunger)
  • Hypotension
  • Dry mucous membranes
  • Skin may be cool (consider Hypothermia)
  • Ketone odor on breath (Acetone smell)
  • Abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting
  • History of polyuria or polydipsia (excessive urination or thirst)
  • Blood glucose determination

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Suction airway as needed
  2. Glucose Check
  3. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. IV NS TKO or INT
  2. Consider 250–500 cc NS bolus, only in patients with signs of dehydration, vomiting or DKA
  3. If BS >250 mg/dL, start 10–20 cc/kg bolus of NS
  4. (peds 4 cc/kg/hr max 150 cc/hr – DO NOT bolus), then reassess blood sugar
