SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

311 – NON-FORMULARY MEDICATIONS

Assessment

  • To provide authorization for the use of medications not commonly used within the current guidelines.
  • For Emergency Use Only.
  • The patient must exhibit the signs and symptoms for which the medication is prescribed.

EMR / EMT / AEMT

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for patient’s condition.
  2. Other treatment will be in accordance with the BLS/ALS SOPs.

Paramedic

  1. Necessary medication(s) administration as requested by caregiver(s):
    1. Caregiver must provide the medication(s) to be administered.
    2. Caregiver must provide a written copy of the physician order and care plan for attachment to the patient care report.
    3. This documentation by the patient’s physician should list the following:
      1. Name of the patient
      2. Name of primary physician
      3. Document must be signed by the primary physician
      4. Contact phone number of the primary physician
      5. Name of the medication(s)
      6. Signs and symptoms for which the medication(s) is prescribed
      7. Dosage of the medication(s)
      8. Number of repeat doses of the medication(s)
      9. Route(s) of administration(s)
      10. Potential side-effects of medication(s)
  2. Medication(s) will only be administered if the patient meets the signs and symptoms for that medication.
  3. Copies of the care plan and physician order must be attached to the patient care report.
  4. If the medication(s) is/are not administered, documentation must include those reasons for withholding.
  5. Whenever medication is administered under these circumstances, transport is mandatory.

Note

  • If you have any additional questions or concerns, please contact Medical Control.
