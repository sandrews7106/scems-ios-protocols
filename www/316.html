SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

316 – UNCONSCIOUS / UNRESPONSIVE / ALTERED MENTAL STATUS

Assessment

  • Unconscious or unresponsive with vital signs
  • Any patient not responding appropriately to verbal or painful stimulus
  • Altered level of consciousness with vital signs
  • Assess for head trauma
  • Assess for hypo/hyperthermia, hemiparesis, fever, OD, hypoglycemia
  • Peds – consider intussusception, intracranial catastrophe, metabolic disorder

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for patient’s condition
  2. Assess for underlying causes and treat accordingly
  3. Narcan IN if suspected opiate overdose (auto-injector or pre-filled device per manufacturer instructions)

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Glucose check

AEMT

  1. IV NS TKO or INT
  2. If hypoglycemic, 12.5–25 g D50 IVP (peds: 2 cc/kg D25 IV/IO)
  3. Narcan 0.4 mg IV/IM/IN/IO titrated to adequate ventilation (peds: <5 y.o – 0.1 mg/kg IV; >5 y.o – 0.4 mg IV), repeatable up to 2 mg

Paramedic

  1. EKG monitor and consider ETCO₂ monitoring
  2. If hypoglycemic and unable to maintain airway, and CVA is not suspected:
    1. 12.5–25 g D50 IVP (peds: 2 cc/kg D25 IV/IO)
    2. Do not administer oral glucose to unresponsive patients
  3. Contact Medical Control for further orders
  4. 20 cc/kg NS fluid challenge (peds: 20 cc/kg)

Note

  • Narcan therapy aims to restore ventilation. Small doses are often effective, especially in chronic opiate users. Larger doses may cause agitation or behavioral symptoms.
