SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

401 – AIR AMBULANCE TRANSPORT

EMR / EMT / AEMT / Paramedic

  1. Request for an Air Ambulance must be in accordance with approved service policy.
  2. A scene flight by air ambulance MAY be indicated IF:
  3. The level I trauma patient’s condition warrants immediate and extreme action and the extrication and/or transport time is greater than 30 minutes and if the patient is not in trauma full arrest.
    1. Transport time is defined as the length of time beginning when the emergency unit would leave the scene transporting until time of arrival at the trauma center.
  4. The on scene Paramedic or EMS Supervisory Personnel shall have the authority to disregard the response of an air ambulance in accordance with approved service policy.
  5. Additional Criteria:
    1. Multi-system blunt or penetrating trauma with unstable vital signs
    2. Greater than 25% TBSA burns
    3. Paralysis or spinal injury
    4. Amputation proximal to wrist or ankle
    5. Flail or crushed chest
    6. Acute MI (STEMI) or stroke who is a candidate for intervention with transport time greater than 30 min.
    7. Pediatric patients requiring urgent specialty care with transport time greater than 30 min.
  6. Situational Criteria:
    1. High energy mechanisms
    2. Prolonged entrapment
    3. Multiple casualty incident
  7. Patients will be categorized according to the current Tennessee Trauma Destination Determinates.
    1. DO NOT request an air ambulance transport if patient is in traumatic cardiopulmonary arrest. If the patient has no vital signs, they are in trauma full-arrest.
    2. The Paramedic in charge of the patient shall have the authority through the Incident Commander to disregard the response of the air ambulance.
    3. The Paramedic will coordinate with the Incident Commander to ensure the helicopter receives patient information and landing zone location.

Note

  • Medical responsibility will be assumed by the medical flight crew personnel upon arrival at the scene.
