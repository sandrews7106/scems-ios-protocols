SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

403 – AVULSED TEETH – STANDING ORDER

Assessment

  • Avulsed teeth may be treated much like small body parts
  • Rinse in normal saline (do not rub or scrub)
  • Place in gauze moistened with saline
  • Do not cool tooth/teeth with ice

EMR / EMT / AEMT

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. C-Spine stabilization
  3. Treat other associated injuries
  4. Monitor airway closely – bleeding and avulsed teeth may cause obstruction
  5. Supportive care
  6. Avulsed teeth may be handled in the same way as small body parts:
    • Rinse in NS (do not scrub)
    • Place in moistened gauze
    • No ice needed

Paramedic

  1. Re-implantation at the scene is recommended to increase chances of successful reattachment. Follow these guidelines:
    1. Permanent teeth only (typically patients over 6.5 years of age)
    2. Only 1–2 teeth cleanly avulsed, with entire root present
    3. Anterior teeth only (front 6, upper or lower)
    4. Patient must be conscious
    5. Attempt within 30 minutes of avulsion
    6. Do not force — gentle insertion only. Slight misalignment can be corrected later
