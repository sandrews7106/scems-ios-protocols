SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

406 – HYPOVOLEMIC SHOCK

Assessment

  • Blood loss due to penetrating injuries to torso or other major vessel
  • Fracture of femur or pelvis
  • G.I. Bleeding, vaginal bleeding, or ruptured ectopic pregnancy
  • Dehydration from vomiting, diarrhea, inadequate intake, fever, DKA, or burns
  • Pulse may be > 120 bpm
  • BP may be < 90 mmHg systolic
  • Orthostatic changes: ↑ pulse by 20 bpm, ↓ SBP by 10 mmHg
  • Severe shock: ↓ LOC, absent radial pulse, cap refill > 2 sec, no palpable BP

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. Consider spinal protection
  3. Control gross hemorrhage – consider tourniquet or clamp
  4. Trendelenburg if no spinal injury suspected

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. IV NS bolus (20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. IV NS or LR x2 large bore, titrate to vitals (target SBP 90–110 mmHg with bleeding)
  3. Consider TXA (2g in 20ml over 1–2 min IV/IO, adult only)
    1. Pharmacology: Inhibits plasminogen/plasmin activity to prevent clot breakdown
    2. Indications:
      1. Bleeding in patients ≥16 y/o
      2. Injury < 3 hrs old
      3. Active hemorrhage
      4. SBP < 90 mmHg or HR > 110 bpm with suspected hemorrhage
    3. Contraindications:
      1. Injuries > 3 hrs old
      2. Evidence of DIC
      3. Use caution with DVT, PE, severe renal failure
      4. Not for pediatric use
  4. Pediatrics:
    1. IV/IO NS 20 cc/kg bolus
    2. Reassess and repeat bolus if needed
    3. Place second IV as needed
    4. Maintain temp > 97°F
  5. Treatment Protocol – Consider:
    1. Dopamine 2–20 µg/kg/min
    2. Epinephrine 2–10 mcg over 1 min (adult only, repeat in 5 min)

Note

  • C-spine immobilization not required for penetrating trauma below nipple line with no spinal/head injury
  • Do not delay transport for immobilization if criteria are met
