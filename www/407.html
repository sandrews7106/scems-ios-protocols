SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

407 – MAJOR THERMAL BURN

Major Burn

  • >20% BSA with partial thickness
  • >10% BSA with full thickness
  • Full thickness burns of head, face, feet, hands, perineum
  • Inhalation or electrical burns
  • Burns with associated trauma
  • Elderly, pediatric, or medically fragile patients

Assessment

  • Remove clothing from affected parts
  • DO NOT pull material from burn site — cut around it
  • Check for facial/airway burns, listen for abnormal breath sounds
  • Was the burn in a closed space?
  • Assess for associated injuries and extent of burns
  • Cardiac monitor for all major burns
  • Evaluate for hypotension, ETOH/drug use, oropharyngeal burns

EMR

  1. Stop burn process with tepid water or saline; remove smoldering clothes
  2. Oxygen and airway support
    1. Watch for airway closure due to edema
    2. Be prepared to assist with BVM
  3. Monitor vitals, reassess frequently
  4. Remove jewelry
  5. Cover burns with dry sterile dressing or burn sheet
  6. Do NOT use Water-Jel or other commercial burn products
  7. Prevent hypothermia
  8. Stabilize other injuries as needed

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. IV NS if hypotensive: 20 cc/kg (peds: 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. Consider pain management protocol
  3. If extremity injured: cover open wounds, splint PRN, avoid movement
  4. Consider sedation with Medical Control guidance (especially peds)
  5. Suspected cyanide exposure: Administer Cyanide Antidote Kit

Notes

  • IV Fluid guide:
    • ≥15 years: 500 mL/hr
    • 5–15 years: 250 mL/hr
    • <5 years: 125 mL/hr
  • Do not overhydrate — may cause third-spacing and worsen shock
