SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

408 – MUSCULOSKELETAL TRAUMA – STANDING ORDER

Assessment

  • Hypotension
  • Past medical history
  • Deformity, swelling, tenderness, crepitus, open or closed fractures
  • Hemorrhaging, lacerations, ecchymosis, instability
  • Decreased function, pulses
  • Loss of sensation of distal extremities
  • ETOH/drug use
  • Mechanism of Injury

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for the patient’s condition
  2. C-Spine protection PRN
  3. Control any life-threatening hemorrhaging, consider a tourniquet or hemorrhage control clamp

EMT

  1. Splint PRN, stabilize penetrating objects
  2. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. INT or IV, LR TKO; if hypotensive, 20 cc/kg (peds 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. Trauma: Isolated extremity trauma only – consider tourniquet, hemorrhage control clamp, and/or hemostatic agent. Clamp is approved for scalp use.
  3. If systolic BP >90 mmHg or peds normal range for age:
    1. Consider pain management protocol
    2. Cover open fractures/lacerations, check distal motor/sensory/pulse pre/post splinting, avoid unnecessary movement
    3. If time allows, administer Ancef:
      1. Ages ≥15: Cefazolin 2g IV drip over ~30 min
      2. Under 15:
        • <40 kg: Cefazolin 25 mg/kg IV over ~30 min
        • ≥40 kg: Cefazolin 1g IV over ~30 min
      3. CONTRAINDICATIONS: Allergy to Cefazolin, cephalosporins, Keflex, or Penicillin
  4. If systolic BP <90 mmHg: IV NS/LR 20 cc/kg (peds: 20 cc/kg)
  5. If patient pregnant (isolated extremity trauma only):
    • If past 1st trimester and systolic BP >90 mmHg – contact Medical Control
    • If systolic BP <90 mmHg – place patient in left lateral recumbent position, IV NS/LR 20 cc/kg

Notes

  • C-spine immobilization is not necessary for penetrating trauma below the nipple line with no spinal/head injury. Do not delay transport for immobilization in these cases.
