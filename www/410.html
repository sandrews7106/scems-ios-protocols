SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

410 – NEUROGENIC SHOCK

Assessment

  • Associated with spinal cord injuries, closed head injuries, and overdoses
  • Signs of hypovolemic shock without pale diaphoretic skin (warm shock)

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for patient’s condition
  2. Establish and maintain C-Spine protection
  3. Hemorrhage control
  4. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. INT or IV NS – if hypotensive, 20 cc/kg (peds: 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit
  2. Treatment – Protocol Consider:
    1. Adult and pediatric – Dopamine at 2–20 µg/kg/min
    2. OR Epinephrine 2–10 mcg over 1 min (Adult only, may repeat in 5 min)

Note

  • Consider occult bleeding and treat as Hypovolemic Shock Protocol
