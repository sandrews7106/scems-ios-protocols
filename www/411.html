SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

411 – SEPTIC SHOCK

Assessment

  • Hot and dry or cool and clammy skin
  • Poor capillary refill
  • Tachycardia/Hypotension
  • Recent history or potential for underlying infection
  • Altered Mental Status

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for patient’s condition
  2. Obtain and record oral or axillary temperature if possible

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Maintain body temperature above 97°F

AEMT

  1. Glucose check
  2. INT or IV NS, if hypotensive 20 cc/kg (peds 20 cc/kg)
  3. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly until normal levels achieved. Try to avoid large swing in serum glucose levels.

Paramedic

  1. ETCO2 Monitoring
  2. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  3. Significant Findings:
    1. Hyperthermia (>101°F)
    2. Hypothermia (<95°F)
    3. Tachypnea (RR >25)
    4. Tachycardia (HR >110)
    5. Acute Mental Status Changes
    6. ETCO2 <25 mmHg
    7. Hypotension (SBP <90 mmHg or MAP <65 mmHg)
    8. Recent or current infection
    9. Patient from Nursing facility
    10. Recent Hospitalization
    11. Cancer Patient
  4. If the patient has 6 or more of these significant findings, begin sepsis alert protocol
  5. Treatment – Protocol:
    1. Administer NS at 30 cc/kg. After 1000 cc has been administered, reassess patient.
    2. Obtain a second large bore IV, if not already. Continue NS bolus.
    3. After 2000 cc reassess patient.
    4. If patient is still hypotensive, administer:
      • Epinephrine (2–10 mcg/min) or
      • Dopamine (5 mcg/kg/min)
      to maintain MAP ≥ 65 in conjunction with saline bolus.
    5. If NS bolus has completed, maintain NS at 100 cc/hr

Note

  • Blood and body fluid precautions: consider isolation, use appropriate PPE (fever and cough or fever and rash). Place surgical mask on patient.
