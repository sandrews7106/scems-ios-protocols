SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

412A – SOFT TISSUE/CRUSH INJURIES

Assessment

  • Hypotension
  • Past medical history
  • Deformity, swelling, tenderness, crepitus, open or closed fractures
  • Hemorrhaging, lacerations, ecchymosis, instability
  • Decreased function, pulses
  • Loss of sensation of distal extremities
  • ETOH/drug use
  • Mechanism of Injury

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for patient’s condition
  2. C-Spine protection PRN
  3. Control any life-threatening hemorrhaging
  4. Consider tourniquet use if appropriate

EMT

  1. Other splints PRN, stabilize penetrating objects
  2. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. INT or IV, NS, if hypotensive 20 cc/kg (peds 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. Trauma: Isolated extremity trauma only – consider tourniquet use. Hemostatic agent may be used on scalp lacerations as well. Elevate affected extremity.
    1. If systolic BP >90 mmHg or peds normal range for age, consider pain management protocol
    2. Cover open fractures/lacerations, check distal motor/sensory/pulse pre/post splinting, avoid unnecessary movement
    3. If systolic BP <90 mmHg, IV NS 20 cc/kg (peds 20 cc/kg)
    4. If patient pregnant: Isolated extremity trauma only
    5. If past 1st trimester and systolic BP <90 mmHg, contact Medical Control
    6. If systolic BP <90 mmHg, place patient in left lateral recumbent position, IV NS LR 20 cc/kg
