SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

412B – CRUSH INJURY / CRUSH SYNDROME

Assessment

  • Crush syndrome results from prolonged compression of large muscle mass (typically >30 min), leading to release of toxic substances like potassium, myoglobin, and acid into circulation upon extrication.

EMR

  1. Airway management (age appropriate)
  2. Supplemental Oxygen

EMT

  1. Pulse ox / Temp

AEMT

  1. ETCO₂ monitoring
  2. Establish IV/IO access
  3. If hypotensive: NS bolus 20cc/kg

Paramedic

  1. Pain control per protocol
  2. Cardiac Monitoring:
  3. Apply 12-lead ECG
    1. Watch for:
      1. Peaked T waves
      2. QRS ≥ 0.12 sec
      3. QT ≥ 0.46 sec
      4. Loss of P wave
      5. Asystole, PEA, VF/VT (go to line b.)
    2. If abnormal ECG or unstable:
      1. Sodium Bicarbonate 50 mEq IV/IO (Peds: 1 mEq/kg)
      2. Calcium Chloride 1g IV/IO over 3 min OR Calcium Gluconate 2g IV/IO
      3. Albuterol 2.5–5 mg neb
  4. IMMEDIATELY BEFORE EXTRICATION:
    1. Sodium Bicarbonate 50 mEq IV/IO (Peds: 1 mEq/kg)
  5. Anticipate cardiac arrest
  6. Continue fluids to maintain SBP > 90 mmHg
  7. Repeat pain control if needed

Notes – Fluid Guidelines Based on Entrapment Time:

  • Time Entrapped <1hr
    • Adult Bolus 500cc / Infusion 1000cc/h
    • Peds Bolus 20cc/kg / Infusion 20cc/kg/h
    • Elderly Bolus 500cc / Infusion 10cc/kg/h
  • Time Entrapped >1hr
    • Adult Bolus 100cc / Infusion 1000cc/h
    • Peds Bolus 20cc/kg / Infusion 20cc/kg/h
    • Elderly Bolus 10cc/kg / Infusion 10cc/kg/h
  • Time Entrapped >2hr
    • Adult Bolus 2000cc / Infusion 1000cc/h
    • Peds Bolus 20cc/kg / Infusion 20cc/kg/h
    • Elderly Bolus 15cc/kg / Infusion 10cc/kg/h

Notes – Considerations

  • Loosely splint if needed
  • Treat hypothermia even in warm settings
  • Rule out and treat other causes of shock
  • Notify medical control early if prolonged extrication is expected
