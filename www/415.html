SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

415 – TENSION PNEUMOTHORAX

Assessment

  • Patient must meet AT LEAST THREE of the below assessment findings to qualify for this standing order, otherwise contact Medical Control
  • Acute respiratory distress, cyanosis
  • Unilaterally decreased or absent breath sounds
  • Hyper-resonance of chest unilaterally
  • Jugular vein distension
  • Subcutaneous emphysema
  • Acute traumatic chest injury, ecchymosis, or obvious rib fractures
  • History of COPD or other chronic lung disease predisposing to spontaneous pneumothorax
  • Hypotension
  • Tracheal deviation away from the affected side
  • Arrhythmia
  • Oxygen saturation < 90%
  • Mechanism of Injury

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate to patient’s condition
  2. Perform frequent evaluation of breath sounds and blood pressure
  3. Control any life-threatening hemorrhaging

EMT

  1. Consider institution of the multiple trauma protocol if indicated (also consider for medical patients)
  2. Follow trauma treatment priority reference as needed
  3. If secondary to sucking chest wound, apply occlusive dressing and treat appropriately
  4. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. IV NS or LR. If hypotensive, give 20 cc/kg (peds 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. If tension pneumothorax suspected, perform needle decompression
    • Use 14g 3.5” needle (pediatric may use 18g)
