SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

416 – TRAUMATIC AMPUTATION(S)

Assessment

  • Hypotension
  • Past medical history
  • Deformity, swelling, tenderness, crepitus, open or closed fractures
  • Hemorrhaging, lacerations, ecchymosis, instability
  • Decreased function, pulses
  • Loss of sensation of distal extremities
  • ETOH/Drug use
  • Mechanism of Injury

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for patient’s condition
  2. C-Spine protection PRN
  3. Control any life-threatening hemorrhaging
    1. Consider tourniquet use if appropriate

EMT

  1. Other splints PRN
  2. Amputated part: If recovered, rinse with NS, wrap in moist dressing, place in plastic bag, and transport with patient
  3. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. INT or IV NS/LR – if hypotensive, 20 cc/kg (peds: 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. Amputation – consider tourniquet use (Note: Use hemostatic agent as last resort)
  3. If systolic BP >90 mmHg or peds within normal range for age, consider pain management protocol
  4. Cover open fractures/lacerations, check distal motor/sensory/pulse pre/post splinting, avoid unnecessary movement
