SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

500A – OB EMERGENCIES REFERENCE

EMR / EMT / AEMT / Paramedic

  • APGAR Score
    • The APGAR score is a rapid method for assessing a newborn at 1 and 5 minutes after birth. It helps determine if immediate medical intervention is needed.
    • Scoring is based on 5 categories. Each is rated 0, 1, or 2 points:

Appearance (Skin Color)

  • 0: Blue, pale
  • 1: Pink body, blue extremities
  • 2: Completely pink

Pulse (Heart Rate)

  • 0: Absent
  • 1: Less than 100 bpm
  • 2: 100 bpm or greater

Grimace (Reflex Irritability)

  • 0: No response
  • 1: Grimace or weak cry
  • 2: Cough, sneeze, or pulls away

Activity (Muscle Tone)

  • 0: Limp
  • 1: Some flexion
  • 2: Active movement

Respiration (Breathing)

  • 0: Absent
  • 1: Slow or irregular
  • 2: Good, strong cry

Interpretation

  • 7–10: Normal — routine care
  • 4–6: Moderately abnormal — may need assistance
  • 0–3: Critically low — immediate resuscitation required

Notes

  • The APGAR score should be calculated after birth of the infant. The five (5) clinical signs are evaluated according to the scoring system detailed above. Each sign is assigned points to be totaled. A total score of 10 indicates that the infant is in the best possible condition. A score of 4 to 6 indicates moderate depression and a need for resuscitative measures.
  • DO NOT delay resuscitation efforts to obtain APGAR score. Obtain APGAR at 1 and 5 minutes after delivery.
