SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

507 – PROLAPSED UMBILICAL CORD

Assessment

  • Cord emerges from the uterus ahead of baby
  • With each uterine contraction the cord is compressed between the presenting part and the pelvis
  • Pulse on exposed cord may or may not be palpable
  • Patient Para (number of live births) and Gravida (number of pregnancies)
  • Term of pregnancy in weeks, EDC
  • Vaginal bleeding
  • Pre-natal medications, problems, and care
  • Membrane ruptured

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for the patient’s condition

EMT

  1. Palpate pulses in the cord
  2. Pulse oximetry
  3. Position the mother with hips elevated
  4. Knee to chest
  5. Hips elevated as much as possible on pillows
  6. Instruct mother to pant with each contraction to prevent her from bearing down
  7. Check for a pulse in the cord
  8. If no pulse – insert a gloved hand into the vagina and gently push the infant’s head off the cord. While maintaining pressure on the head, cover the exposed cord with a sterile dressing moistened in saline. Transport immediately and DO NOT remove your hand until relieved by hospital staff.
  9. If pulse present – cover exposed cord with moist dressing
  10. Contact Medical Control as soon as possible if time and patient condition allows

AEMT

  1. IV NS TKO, if hypotensive 20 cc/kg (peds 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG if appropriate
