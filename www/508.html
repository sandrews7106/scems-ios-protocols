SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

508 – PRE-ECLAMPSIA AND ECLAMPSIA

Assessment

  1. Patient Para (number of live births) and Gravida (number of pregnancies)
  2. Term of pregnancy in weeks, EDC
  3. Vaginal bleeding
  4. Pre-natal medications, problems, and care
  5. Membrane ruptured
  6. Usually begins after the twentieth week of pregnancy
  7. Most often affects women during their first pregnancy
  8. May have a history of chronic hypertension and/or diabetes
  9. May experience hypertension and edema
  10. May experience headaches, blurred vision, and abdominal pain
  11. May experience seizures which indicates a progression from pre-eclampsia to eclampsia

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for the patient’s condition
  2. Place patient in left lateral recumbent position

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry

AEMT

  1. Glucose check
  2. IV NS TKO, if hypotensive 20 cc/kg (peds 20 cc/kg)

Paramedic

  1. EKG monitor
  2. Valium 5 mg slow IV PRN or Versed 2–5 mg or Ativan 1–2 mg IVP per seizure protocol if generalized seizure activity
  3. Contact Medical Control and consider: Magnesium Sulfate 1–2 grams IV Slowly

NOTE

  • Record a blood pressure and the presence or absence of edema in every pregnant woman you examine no matter what the chief complaint
