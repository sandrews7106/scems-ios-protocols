SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

601 – DISCONTINUATION / WITHHOLDING OF LIFE SUPPORT

EMR EMT AEMT Paramedic

  • Once life support has been initiated in the field, Non ALS personnel CAN NOT discontinue resuscitative measures unless directed to do so by the on-scene physician, EMT-Paramedic or presented with a valid Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment (POST/DNR).
  1. Withholding Resuscitation – Standing Orders:
    1. If there is no CPR in progress, CPR may be withheld if one or more of the following conditions are met:
      1. Obviously dead patients with dependent lividity, rigor mortis, or massive trauma (e.g., evacuation of the cranial vault, crushed chest, crushed head).
      2. Obviously dead patients with tissue decomposition.
      3. Patients without vital signs who cannot be accessed for treatment due to entrapment for prolonged time (12–15 minutes or greater).
      4. Severe blunt trauma with absence of BP, pulse, respiratory effort, neurologic response, and pupillary response.
      5. Presented with a valid POST/DNR order or copy, as approved by the Tennessee Department of Health.
      6. Instructed to do so by the on scene Paramedic.
  2. Discontinuing Life Support
    1. Once life support has been initiated in the field, in order to discontinue it, the following conditions must be met:
      1. Discontinuation of CPR and ALS intervention may be implemented prior to contact with Medical Control if ALL of the following are met:
        1. Patient must be 18 years of age or older.
        2. Adequate CPR has been administered.
        3. Airway managed with proper verification (intubation, BIAD, cricothyrotomy, or BVM with End Tidal CO₂).
        4. IV or IO access achieved.
        5. No evidence or suspicion of:
          • Drug/toxin overdose
          • Active internal bleeding
          • Hypothermia
          • Preceding trauma
        6. 3 cycles of drug therapy administered per protocol without return of spontaneous circulation.
        7. All EMS Paramedic personnel agree that discontinuation is appropriate.
      2. If all above are not met and discontinuation is desired, contact Medical Control.
  3. Field Termination
    1. Upon termination, all tubes, needles and IV lines must remain in place (tie off and cut IV lines with catheter left in place).

NOTES

  • Use this standing order with careful consideration. Conditions like overdose, electrical shock, hypothermia, and hypoglycemia may mimic some signs and symptoms.
  • All deaths must be confirmed by a Paramedic.
