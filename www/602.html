SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

602 – FIELD DETERMINATION OF DEATH

Assessment

  • Pulseless, non-breathing with definitive signs of death:
  • Rigor Mortis
  • Dependent lividity
  • Decomposition of body tissue
  • Devastating, un-survivable injury:
    • Decapitation
    • Incineration
    • Separation of vital internal organ(s) from the body or total destruction of organs
    • Gunshot wound to the head that crosses the midline (entrance and exit)

EMR / EMT / AEMT / Paramedic

  1. If patient is pulseless, non-breathing without definitive signs of death:
    1. Must receive resuscitation unless a properly executed DNR or POST form is present.

Treatment – Standing Order

  1. DNR Orders:
    1. If family member or caregiver can produce a properly executed DNR or POST order, resuscitation can be withheld.
    2. Treat patients with known DNR orders appropriately; just do not initiate CPR if they develop cardiovascular or respiratory arrest.
    3. When there is any doubt about what to do, begin resuscitative efforts with all skill available.
  2. Resuscitation has been initiated prior to EMS arrival:
    1. Anytime CPR or an attempt at resuscitation has been initiated by anyone at the scene, resuscitative efforts will be continued until:
      • Medical Control directs the team to stop (either online or on-scene), or
      • It is determined the patient meets criteria for “definitive signs” of death, or
      • A properly executed DNR or POST form is presented
