SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

606 – PHYSICIAN ON SCENE

  1. If private physician intervenes by phone the EMT-Basic/EMT-Paramedic shall:
    1. Request the physician contact Medical Control and relay any orders through them.
    2. NO ORDERS will be taken over the phone from the private physician.
  2. Standing Order:
    1. No one will be recognized as a physician without proof of license. This must be in the form of a wallet card or visual personal recognition. NO ORDERS will be accepted until proof of license is verified.
    2. Consider need for Law Enforcement if any difficulty with person occurs.
    3. The EMT or above shall:
      1. Inform the physician that they must contact Medical/Trauma Control.
      2. Inform Medical/Trauma Control of the presence of a physician on scene.
    4. Medical/Trauma Control may:
      1. Speak to the physician to determine the qualifications.
      2. Request the EMT, AEMT, or Paramedic to verify licensure of the physician.
      3. Relinquish total responsibility for the patient to the on-scene physician.
    5. Physician (intervening) may:
      1. Assist the EMS Provider and allow operation under EMS standing orders and protocols.
      2. Request to talk to Medical/Trauma Control to offer advice and assistance.
      3. Take responsibility for care given if approved by Medical/Trauma Control, and physically accompany the patient to the ED, signing for all instructions.
      4. Contact should be made with Medical/Trauma Control if this happens.
    6. If private physician intervenes by phone or in person, the EMS provider shall:
      1. Inform the physician that EMS must contact Medical/Trauma Control.
      2. Request the physician contact Medical Control and relay any orders through them.
      3. NO ORDERS should be taken over the phone from the private physician. Orders only via Medical/Trauma Control.
