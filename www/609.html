SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

609 – SPINAL MOTION RESTRICTION

EMR EMT AEMT Paramedic

  1. Guideline Intent: Decrease injury and discomfort caused by unnecessary immobilization.
    1. Backboards may cause harm: pain, agitation, ulcers, respiratory compromise.
    2. Use in CPR is acceptable.
  2. Spinal Injury Assessment:
    1. Perform SMR if unstable spinal injury suspected (esp. peds or elderly).
    2. Penetrating trauma (e.g. GSW, stab wound) → No SMR unless spine signs.
    3. Highest-level provider determines need for or removal of immobilization.
    4. If already immobilized prior to assessment, reassess before proceeding.
  3. Spinal Motion Restriction (SMR):
    1. Reduce gross movement.
    2. Prevent spine reinjury.
    3. Reassess motor/sensory function regularly.

Procedure

  1. Scene Assessment: Risk-based, not mechanism-only.
    1. Higher-risk: MVCs, axial loading, torso injuries, falls >10 ft
  2. Initial Patient Assessment: In current position.
  3. SMR Criteria: Apply C-collar if:
    1. Midline spinal pain/tenderness
    2. AMS or neurologic deficit
    3. Intoxication
    4. Distracting injury
    5. Torticollis (children)
    6. Communication barrier
  4. If none apply → no collar.
    1. Penetrating neck injuries: Do not immobilize
    2. Extrication options:
      1. Let patients self-extricate when possible
      2. Use lift-slide or vacuum device if needed
  5. Transport:
    1. Avoid backboards unless absolutely necessary
    2. Use padding, vacuum mattress if needed
    3. SMR applies to entire spine
    4. Cervical collar = critical for cervical SMR
  6. Adverse Effects:
    1. Use alternative positioning or equipment if SMR poorly tolerated
    2. Acceptable positions: supine, semi-Fowler’s, lateral, etc.
    3. Use pillows, child seats, scoop, vacuum mattress, etc.
    4. Self-limited motion acceptable in cooperative patients
    5. Soft collars or padding acceptable if rigid collar not tolerated
  7. Movement Considerations:
    1. Self-extrication preferred
    2. Limit spine motion during moves
    3. Use scoops/pull sheets as needed
  8. Standing Take Downs: Not recommended; allow patient to sit on stretcher.
  9. Padding & Reassessment:
    1. Reassess: motor, sensory, SpO₂, EtCO₂
    2. Protect airway
    3. Minimize motion during transfer

Special Considerations

  1. Respiratory Difficulty: Use SMR cautiously in dyspnea cases.
  2. Pediatrics <9 years:
    1. Use padded board
    2. Avoid agitation-inducing techniques
    3. If in car seat, assess posterior if choosing SMR in-seat
  3. Combative Patients: Avoid provoking increased motion.
  4. Car Seats:
    1. Infants in car seats: Leave in if SMR secure and airway stable
    2. Booster seats: Remove and apply traditional SMR
  5. Helmet Removal:
    1. Remove if:
      1. No shoulder pads
      2. Facial/head trauma where faceplate insufficient
      3. Advanced airway needed
      4. Helmet is loose
      5. Cardiac arrest (remove both helmet and pads)
    2. If helmet/pads mismatched → pad missing side
    3. Remove all other helmets to maintain spinal alignment
