SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

612 - EXCITED DELIRIUM

Assessment

  • Changes in LOC
  • Ongoing Disorientation
  • Agitation
  • Hallucination
  • Hyperthermia
  • Seizure
  • Chest Pain or difficulty breathing
  • Significant injury from fall or takedown

EMR

  1. Oxygen and airway maintenance appropriate for patient’s condition
  2. Supportive care

EMT

  1. Pulse oximetry
  2. Glucose Check

AEMT

  1. IV LR or NS, bolus (20 cc/kg)
  2. Titrate Dextrose 50% PRN slowly until normal levels achieved. Try to avoid large swing in serum glucose levels.

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG if appropriate
  2. Valium 2–10 mg slow IVP PRN, or
  3. Versed 2–5 mg IVP if generalized seizure activity, or
  4. Ativan 1–2 mg IV, or
  5. Ketamine 2 mg/kg IM or IV over one minute (Adult Only)
  6. Consider RSI as last resort if above ineffective and remains danger to self or others.

NOTES

  • All persons subjected to use of law enforcement taser device should be medically evaluated and monitored regularly.
  • SCEMS does not remove taser barbs in the field.
  • Darts to eyes, mouth, face, neck and genitals or near indwelling medical devices should be monitored closely.
