SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

701 – MAINTENANCE OF BLOOD OR BLOOD PRODUCTS

Purpose

  • Establish guidelines for the administration of blood.

Procedure

  1. Obtain type & cross-matched PRBCs and transfusion orders from referring facility.
  2. Referring physician must explain transfusion risks/benefits and document consent.
  3. Verify patient ID and blood unit match by two providers using hospital wristband.
  4. Administer through a blood filter and primary NS infusion.
  5. Record baseline vitals.
  6. Check vitals 5 minutes after starting and then at least every 15 minutes.
  7. Paramedics may maintain pre-established infusions during transfer. If multiple units are required, continue as needed unless complications arise.
  8. After final unit infuses:
    1. Provide cardiac monitoring
    2. Oxygen therapy
    3. Confirm flow rate/settings
    4. Receive premixed Dopamine from facility
    5. Verify rate with MD order (written/verbal)
  9. Document unit number, vitals (including temp), and reaction signs.
  10. Post-transfusion:
    1. Spike NS and run at KVO or to maintain BP
    2. Document time and volume infused
    3. Return used bag to receiving facility
    4. Document:
      1. Start/stop time
      2. Lot number and patient MRN

Identification and Management of Complications

  1. Hemolytic Transfusion Reaction
    1. Signs: flushing, hyperventilation, hives, fever, dark urine, impending doom
    2. Management:
      1. Stop transfusion, change tubing, start NS, save blood bag for lab
      2. Maintain normovolemia
      3. Consider Lasix 0.5–1.0 mg/kg or Mannitol 12.5 g IV
      4. If hypotensive: Dopamine 2–5 mcg/kg/min
  2. Febrile Non-Hemolytic Reaction
    1. Signs: headache, chills, fever
    2. Management:
      1. Stop transfusion, change tubing, start NS, save bag for lab
      2. Benadryl 25–50 mg IV
      3. Acetaminophen 325 mg PO
  3. Anaphylactic Reaction
    1. Signs: hives, hypotension, tachycardia, wheezing
    2. Management:
      1. Stop transfusion, change tubing, bolus NS if hypotensive
      2. Epinephrine 0.3–0.5 mg SQ/IM (1:1000) adult, 0.01 mg/kg child
      3. For severe cases: Epi 0.3–0.5 mg IV (1:10,000)
      4. Benadryl 25–50 mg IV
      5. Consider intubation for airway compromise
  4. Circulatory Overload
    1. Signs: dyspnea, orthopnea, HTN, CHF
    2. Management:
      1. Stop transfusion
      2. Sit patient upright
      3. Consider Lasix 0.5–1.0 mg/kg IV
