SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

704 – CPAP / MECHANICAL VENTILATION

Rationale

  • Continuous Positive Airway Pressure has been shown to improve vital signs, gas exchange, reduce the work of breathing, decrease dyspnea, and the need for intubation in asthma, COPD, pulmonary edema, CO poisoning, near drowning, CHF, and pneumonia.
  • AEMTs may initiate CPAP if ALS care isn’t immediately available in a patient with severe distress.

Indications

  • Respiratory distress not due to trauma or pneumothorax
  • Awake and able to follow commands
  • Age >12 with properly fitting CPAP mask
  • Maintains open airway
  • SBP > 90 mmHg
  • Accessory muscle use during respirations
  • Symptoms of asthma, COPD, pulmonary edema, CHF, or pneumonia
  • Consider for severe respiratory distress with DNR AND 2+ of:
    • RR > 25
    • SpO₂ < 94%
    • Accessory muscle use

Contraindications

  • Apnea or respiratory arrest
  • Suspected pneumothorax or chest trauma
  • Tracheostomy
  • Active vomiting or upper GI bleeding
  • Impaired airway protection or obtundation
  • Facial trauma or deformity preventing seal

Precautions

  1. Use care in patients with:
    1. Impaired mental status
    2. Failed prior non-invasive ventilation
    3. Active or recent upper GI bleeding
    4. Nausea or vomiting
    5. Inadequate respiratory effort
    6. Excessive secretions
    7. Facial deformity preventing mask fit

Notes

  • For ventilated patients with ETT/Trach, consider sedation and pain control

Non-Invasive Positive Pressure Ventilation (NIPPV) – Hamilton T1

Initial Settings:

  1. Circuit leak check
  2. Apply capnography before placing mask
  3. Consider inline bronchodilators and sedation if anxious
  4. CPAP:
    1. IPAP: 0 cm H₂O
    2. EPAP: 5 cm H₂O
  5. BiPAP:
    1. IPAP: 12 cm H₂O
    2. EPAP: 6 cm H₂O
  6. Monitor tidal volume to avoid barotrauma

Mechanical Ventilation

  1. Circuit leak check
  2. Determine Ideal Body Weight (IBW)
    1. Tidal volume: 8 mL/kg
    2. For lung injury: 4–6 mL/kg
    3. Do not use actual body weight
  3. Confirm tube placement with capnography
  4. Input vent settings prior to starting
  5. Confirm placement again after vent started
  6. Adjust settings in 2 cm H₂O increments
    1. Typical settings:
      1. TV: 8 mL/kg
      2. Mode: SIMV/AC/CMV
      3. Rate: 12 bpm
      4. PEEP: 5 cm H₂O
      5. FiO₂: 1.0 (100%)
  7. Continuously monitor sedation, analgesia, and respiratory status
