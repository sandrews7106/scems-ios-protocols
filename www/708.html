SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

708 – ENDOTRACHEAL TUBE INTRODUCER (BOUGIE)

Paramedic

Indications

  • Clinical indications for oral intubation (Bougie may be used with any attempt)
  • Anticipated or predictable difficult airway

Contraindications

  • Introducer diameter larger than ETT internal diameter

Procedure

  1. Prepare, position, and oxygenate the patient with 100% oxygen
  2. Select proper ETT (without stylet), test cuff, and prepare suction
  3. Lubricate distal half of both the Bougie and ETT
  4. Perform laryngoscopy and visualize cords if possible (use Sellick’s/BURP if needed)
  5. Insert Bougie with curved tip anteriorly, advance through vocal cords or above arytenoids if cords not visible
  6. Advance until you meet resistance or “hold-up” (lack of resistance suggests esophageal placement)
  7. Withdraw slightly to load ETT, keeping control of Bougie
  8. Re-advance Bougie and loaded ETT until “hold-up” confirms tracheal placement
  9. Advance ETT over Bougie to correct depth
  10. If resistance encountered, rotate ETT 90° counter-clockwise and attempt again; laryngoscopy may assist if needed
  11. Secure ETT and remove Bougie
  12. Confirm placement, inflate cuff (3–10 cc), auscultate, and reposition if necessary
  13. Secure tube, apply end-tidal CO₂, document placement and waveform confirmation
