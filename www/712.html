SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

712 – HEMORRHAGE CONTROL CLAMP

Paramedic

Indications

  • Temporary control of severe bleeding in scalp, extremities, axilla, or inguinal areas

Contraindications

  • Do not use where skin cannot be approximated (e.g., large skin defects under high tension)

Warnings and Precautions

  • Temporary use only – not to exceed 3 hours
  • Device removal and wound repair must be done by medical personnel
  • Use as directed to avoid needlestick injury
  • Do not use near delicate structures <10mm below surface (e.g., eyes)
  • Not effective for internal hemorrhage (e.g., abdomen or chest)
  • Wear full PPE to prevent blood splash exposure
  • Single-use only – discard if packaging is damaged
  • Dispose of as a sharps device
  • Consider tourniquet for severe extremity injuries where clamp use is not feasible

Procedure

  1. Explain procedure to conscious patient
  2. Don appropriate PPE
  3. Open sterile packaging by pulling outer tabs
  4. Lift device carefully from package – avoid premature closure
    1. If device closes accidentally, press side buttons inward and pull arms apart
  5. Identify wound edges
  6. Align device lengthwise with wound; position needles 1–2 cm from edge on both sides
  7. Close arms to apply clamp – safety seal will break
  8. Wipe wound area with gauze to check for bleeding; apply more than one clamp if needed
  9. If bleeding persists:
    1. Re-check clamp position and apply more pressure to arms
    2. Use additional clamps for large wounds

Removal

  1. Repositioning may be performed in the field; otherwise, removal must be done in-hospital
    1. Grip bars, press clamp further closed to release lock
    2. While pressing arms, press both release buttons
    3. Pull one bar open while rotating needle from wound
    4. Handle clamp by buttons only to avoid contact with needles
    5. Dispose of per sharps disposal guidelines

Notes

  • Hemostatic agents and/or wound packing may be used under the clamp – do not remove them prior to clamp application
