SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

713 – INDWELLING IV PORT ACCESS

Paramedic

Indications

  1. IV fluids or medications emergently needed AND
  2. No peripheral IV access AND
  3. Patient exhibits:
    1. Indwelling port present
    2. Altered mental status (GCS ≤ 8)
    3. Respiratory compromise (SaO₂ ≤ 80% post O₂ and/or RR <10 or >40/min)
    4. Hemodynamic instability

Contraindications

  1. Infection at insertion site
  2. Significant edema
  3. Excess tissue at site
  4. Landmarks not palpable

Considerations

  1. Field access is for EMERGENCY use only.
  2. Only by trained personnel with documented competency.
  3. Use patient's own supplies if needed and safe.
  4. DO NOT FORCE FLUSH INDWELLING CATHETERS.

Procedure

  1. Accessing the Port
    1. Gather supplies:
      1. 10cc NS syringe
      2. Chloraprep
      3. Masks (crew and patient)
      4. Sterile gloves
      5. Huber needle w/ extension tubing
      6. Transpore tape
      7. IV/NS setup
    2. Hand hygiene
    3. Peel back one corner of Huber needle package; extend tubing only
    4. Attach syringe, prime tubing/needle
    5. Place kit on flat surface, open fully without touching needle
    6. Apply masks (caregiver and optionally patient)
    7. Don sterile gloves
    8. Prep skin with 30-sec Chloraprep scrub; allow to dry
    9. Pick up needle (do not touch syringe)
    10. Remove protective sheath
    11. Stabilize port with non-dominant hand (“V” grip)
    12. Insert needle at 90° angle into port reservoir
    13. Do not twist needle once seated
    14. Flush with 2–5cc NS, then aspirate for blood return
    15. Inject remaining NS slowly; assess for resistance/swelling
    16. If resistance persists, remove and reattempt if needed
    17. Remove syringe and connect IV tubing
    18. Apply pressure with 2x2 as needed (bleeding should be minimal)
  2. Dressing the Site
    1. Gather supplies:
      1. CVC dressing kit
      2. Flat clean surface
    2. Optional: place 2x2 under needle for padding
    3. Secure with split tape in an "X" pattern
    4. Cover with transpore tape
    5. Tape down excess tubing
