SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

715 – MECHANICAL CPR DEVICE

Inclusion Criteria

  • The device must be present on scene within 8 minutes of the initiation of CPR.
  • The patient must not meet any of the exclusion criteria.

Exclusion Criteria

  • Body habitus too large for the device.
  • Children <42 kg / 90 lbs or any individual for whom the suction cup does not make firm contact with the chest wall.

Procedure

  1. If the above inclusion criteria are met, none of the exclusion criteria are present, and a mechanical device is available, the following steps will be taken to implement its use:
    1. The mechanical CPR device should be applied as soon as possible.
    2. Defibrillation performed if indicated.
    3. CPR resumed using the Mechanical CPR Device.
    4. Obtain airway (adequate ventilation with OPA/NPA/BVM, King Airway, or ETT).
    5. IV/IO access.
    6. Initiation of ACLS medications.
    7. Allow at least 90 seconds of CPR after any medications given before pausing to check rhythm.
    8. If pulse confirmed, prepare for immediate transport. The Mechanical CPR device may be turned off but must remain on the patient during transport to the hospital.
    9. If the patient goes back into cardiac arrest, immediately resume mechanical CPR and continue ACLS.
    10. Detailed documentation with times of all initiation and termination of use of the Mechanical CPR device must be kept for statistical and feedback purposes.
    11. Trained personnel from the agency providing the device must be present during device usage (including transport).
