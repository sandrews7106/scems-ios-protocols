SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

716 – PPE DONNING PROCEDURE

Sullivan County EMS PPE Donning Procedure

  • This process requires complete attention to detail and should be performed in "slow motion" at slower than normal speed. The Deputy Chief / shift supervisor should assign the trained observer "TO" for each step, with employees strictly following instructions. The trained observer should verify procedure and compliance to avoid contamination.
  1. Gather all supplies.
  2. Engage Trained Observer
  3. Remove all jewelry, makeup, chewing gum, candy, nonessential clothing, etc.
  4. Inspect PPE prior to donning.
  5. Perform hand hygiene. (hand sanitizer)
  6. Apply long cuff nitrile care gloves. (inner gloves)
  7. Apply (put on) Tyvek suit with hood folded inside. (Yellow)
  8. Apply boot covers (must extend up to mid-calf)
  9. Apply long gown. TO will tie in back.
  10. Ensure hair is secured and confined (blue bonnet) prior to donning N-95.
  11. Apply N-95, seal mask to the face ensuring straps are not crossed and properly located at the crown of the head and the base of neck.
  12. Perform a fit check of respirator, breathing deeply in and out, feeling with your hands for any air leakage.
  13. Apply head cover, ensure hood covers all hair, ears, and neck.
  14. Apply long cuff nitrile gloves pulling over gown cuff to ensure no exposed skin.
  15. Apply apron (plastic) for extra protection against fluids.
  16. Apply face shield over the head cover
  17. Verify the integrity of PPE by bending, reaching and performing a thorough range of motion to assure all areas of the body remain covered by PPE during movements.
  18. Disinfect outer gloves with hand sanitizer and allow to dry prior to patient contact.
  19. Perform frequent disinfection of gloved hands with hand sanitizer after handling body fluids or contaminated items.
  20. If a partial or total breach occurs, move immediately to doffing area to disinfect and assess exposure.

Notes

  • T.O. Should wear identical equipment from the neck down. N95, head cover and face shield should be readily available and donned if needed.
  • While working in PPE caring for Ebola patients, Health Care Workers (HCW) should have no skin exposed.
  • Do not add a third pair of gloves.
  • Disinfect immediately any visibly contaminated PPE surfaces, equipment or patient care surfaces using a disinfectant wipe.
  • Bleach wipes or hand sanitizer may be used to decontaminate the long cuff nitrile gloves if necessary.
