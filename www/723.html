SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

723 – PAIN MANAGEMENT

Assessment

  • Severe pain from traumatic injuries
  • Burns
  • Medical Emergencies requiring pain management
  • Sickle Cell Anemia

EMR/EMT

  1. Oxygen to maintain a SPO2 of 94%

AEMT

  1. IV NS bolus 20 cc/kg

Paramedic

  1. 12 Lead EKG, transmit if appropriate
  2. Place patient on capnography prior to administering pain management if time allows but the patient MUST be on after medication administration
  3. Consider Fentanyl, Morphine or Ketamine depending on how hemodynamically stable the patient is
  4. Dosing as follows (may be repeated once if hemodynamically stable prior to consulting with medical control):
    • Adults
      1. Fentanyl: 50mcg–100mcg IV/IM (MAX one time dose of 100mcg) or 2mcg/kg IN (Repeated 15min)
      2. Morphine: 5mg–10mg IV/IM with a MAX one time dose of 10mg
      3. Ketamine: 0.1–0.3mg/kg IV/IM (may be used in conjunction with another analgesic)
    • Pediatrics
      1. Fentanyl: 1mcg/kg IV/IM/IO or 2mcg/kg IN
      2. Morphine: 0.1mg/kg IV/IO
      3. Ketamine: 0.1–0.3mg/kg IV/IM

Notes

  • Consider half doses in geriatric patients
