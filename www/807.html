SULLIVAN COUNTY EMS PROTOCOLS

807 – TRAUMA ASSESSMENT / DESTINATION GUIDELINES

EMR EMT AEMT PARAMEDIC

  • Perform primary and secondary survey
  • Treat any life threatening injuries/illness
  • Obtain vital signs
  • Determine mechanism of injury
  • Obtain past medical history
Is transport to Trauma Center >30 minutes?
YES
• Initiate transport to closest appropriate facility. Notify Medical Control of decision
• Transport to trauma center may exceed 30 minutes if dictated by local Medical Control or Trauma Control
• Medical Control will have final jurisdiction over destination, excluding:
- Any patient of legal majority (age 18 or over, the parent or legal guardian of a minor patient, or an emancipated minor) shall have the right to request transportation to specific facility within the region of origin
- Transport of the patient shall not be delayed by family/supervisor requests if transport decisions are made based on patient care needs 		NO
TRANSPORT TO LEVEL I TRAUMA CENTER IF:
• GCS <13 and/or
• Systolic BP <90 mmHg
• Respiratory rate <10 or >30

TRANSPORT TO LEVEL I TRAUMA CENTER IF:
• Penetrating injury proximal to elbow or knee
• Flail chest
• Large open chest wound (sucking chest wound)
• Partial/complete amputation proximal to wrist or ankle
• Combination trauma with burns of >15% BSA, or to face/head/airway
• 2 or more long bone fractures
• Pelvic fracture
• Tension pneumothorax
• Patient ejection from the vehicle

CONTACT TRAUMA CONTROL TO CONSIDER TRANSPORT TO LEVEL I, II, III TRAUMA CENTER IF:
• High-speed accident with intrusion of >12”
• Ejection (vehicle or auto vs. pedestrian)
• Motorcycle crash >20 MPH
• Fall >20 feet

CONTACT TRAUMA CONTROL TO CONSIDER TRANSPORT TO LEVEL I TRAUMA CENTER IF:
• Known cancer, respiratory disease or psychosis
• Known cardiac, diabetic, or dialysis
• Known chronic arthropathy, malignancy, obesity or coagulopathy

If the patient’s condition deteriorates during transport, such that their life/health are considered in serious jeopardy, AND the specifically planned destination is prudent, AND if Medical Control deems transport to a higher level trauma center is necessary, the patient may be transported to the appropriate facility.

  • Early notification is imperative when the patient is being considered for Trauma Alert.
  • Trauma control is considered to be any Emergency Physician within the local trauma system.
  • Any unmanageable trauma patient is justification for transport to closest appropriate facility.
  • Otherwise, the patient should be transported according to the state destination guidelines.

Any trauma patient that is transported to a Level I, II, or III trauma facility should have documentation that justifies the transport. Example, the patient was transported to Level III facility as they were unstable and lengthy transport would have endangered the patient, or the patient deteriorated during transport requiring destination change.
