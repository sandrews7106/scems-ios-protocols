MEDICATION & FLUID FORMULAS

Common Metric Conversions

  • 1 L = 1000 mL
  • 1 mg = 1000 mcg
  • 1 g = 1000 mg
  • 1 kg = 2.2 lbs
  • 1 mL = 1 cc

Use the following formulas for calculating drug dosages, IV rates, and infusions.

1. Weight Conversion

\[ \frac{\text{lbs}}{2.2} = \text{kg} \]

Weight: 175 lbs

\[ \frac{175}{2.2} = 79.5 \approx 80\, \text{kg} \]

2. Basic Volume Calculation (non-weight-based)

\[ \frac{\text{DO} \times \text{V}}{\text{DH}} = \text{mL} \]

Order: 2 mg
DH: 10 mg/mL
Volume (V): 1 mL

\[ \frac{2 \times 1}{10} = 0.2\, \text{mL} \]

3. Volume to Administer (mL)

\[ \frac{\text{DO} \times \text{wt.} \times \text{V}}{\text{DH}} = \text{mL} \]

Order: 2 mg/kg IV
Weight: 80 kg
Drug Concentration (DH): 100 mg/mL
Volume (V): 1 mL

\[ \frac{2 \times 80 \times 1}{100} = 1.6\, \text{mL} \]

4. IV Drip Rate (gtt/min)

\[ \frac{\text{Volume} \times \text{Drop Factor}}{\text{Time}} = \text{gtt/min} \]

Volume: 500 mL
Drop Factor: 15 gtt/mL
Time: 30 min

\[ \frac{500 \times 15}{30} = 250\, \text{gtt/min} \]

5. IV Pump Infusion (mL/hr)

\[ \frac{\text{Dose} \times \text{wt.} \times min \times \text{V}}{\text{DH}} = \text{mL/hr} \]

Dose: 5 mcg/kg/min
Weight: 70 kg
Time: 60 min (1 hr)
DH: 1600 mcg/mL

\[ \frac{5 \times 70 \times 60 \times 1}{1600} = 13.1\, \text{mL/hr} \]

6. Weight-Based Medication Infusion Without Pump (gtt/min)

\[\frac{\text{DO} \times \text{wt.} \times \text{mL} \times \text{gtt factor}}{\text{DH}} = \text{gtt/min}\]

Drug Ordered (DO): 5 mcg/kg/min
Weight: 70 kg
Volume: 250 mL
Drop Factor: 60 gtt/mL
DH (Total Drug in Solution): 400,000 mcg

\[\frac{5 \times 70 \times 250 \times 60}{400000} = 13.1\, \text{gtt/min}\]

7. Non-weight-based Medication Infusion (gtt/min)

\[ \frac{\text{DO} \times \text{V} \times \text{gtt factor}}{\text{DH}} = \text{gtt/min} \]

Order: 10 mg
DH: 20 mg/mL
Volume (V): 1 mL
Drop Factor: 60 gtt/mL

\[ \frac{10 \times 1 \times 60}{20} = 30\, \text{gtt/min} \]

8. Parkland Formula (Burn Fluid Resuscitation)

\[ 4 \times \text{kg} \times \%\text{TBSA} = \text{mL over 24 hrs} \]

Give half over the first 8 hours from time of injury.

Weight: 70 kg
Burn Surface Area: 40%

\[ 4 \times 70 \times 40 = 11200\, \text{mL in 24 hrs} \]

First 8 hrs: 5600 mL

9. Consensus Formula (Burns)

\[ 2 \times \text{kg} \times \%\text{TBSA} = \text{mL over 24 hrs} \]

Used for adults with partial/full thickness burns.

Weight: 70 kg
TBSA: 40%

\[ 2 \times 70 \times 40 = 5600\, \text{mL in 24 hrs} \]
