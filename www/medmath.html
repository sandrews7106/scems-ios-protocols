MEDICATION & FLUID FORMULAS

Common Metric Conversions

  • 1 L = 1000 mL
  • 1 mg = 1000 mcg
  • 1 g = 1000 mg
  • 1 kg = 2.2 lbs
  • 1 mL = 1 cc

Use the following formulas for calculating drug dosages, IV rates, and infusions.

1. Volume to Administer (mL)

\[ \frac{\text{DO} \times \text{wt.} \times \text{V}}{\text{DH}} = \text{mL} \]

Order: 2 mg/kg IV
Weight: 80 kg
Drug Concentration (DH): 100 mg/mL
Volume (V): 1 mL

\[ \frac{2 \times 80 \times 1}{100} = 1.6\, \text{mL} \]

2. IV Drip Rate (gtt/min)

\[ \frac{\text{Volume} \times \text{Drop Factor}}{\text{Time}} = \text{gtt/min} \]

Volume: 500 mL
Drop Factor: 15 gtt/mL
Time: 30 min

\[ \frac{500 \times 15}{30} = 250\, \text{gtt/min} \]

3. Infusion Rate (mL/hr)

\[ \frac{\text{Dose} \times \text{wt.} \times 60 \times \text{V}}{\text{DH}} = \text{mL/hr} \]

Dose: 5 mcg/kg/min
Weight: 70 kg
DH: 1600 mcg/mL

\[ \frac{5 \times 70 \times 60 \times 1}{1600} = 13.1\, \text{mL/hr} \]

4. Dose from Premixed Infusion (mcg/kg/min)

\[ \frac{\text{Rate} \times \text{DH}}{\text{wt.} \times 60 \times \text{V}} = \text{mcg/kg/min} \]

Rate: 15 mL/hr
DH: 1600 mcg/mL
Weight: 80 kg

\[ \frac{15 \times 1600}{80 \times 60} = 5\, \text{mcg/kg/min} \]

5. Pediatric Dose (mg or mcg)

\[ \text{DO} \times \text{wt.} = \text{Total Dose} \]

Order: 0.1 mg/kg
Weight: 15 kg

\[ 0.1 \times 15 = 1.5\, \text{mg} \]

6. Weight Conversion

\[ \frac{\text{lbs}}{2.2} = \text{kg} \]

Weight: 175 lbs

\[ \frac{175}{2.2} = 79.5 \approx 80\, \text{kg} \]

7. Basic Volume Calculation (non-weight-based)

\[ \frac{\text{DO} \times \text{V}}{\text{DH}} = \text{mL} \]

Order: 2 mg
DH: 10 mg/mL
Volume (V): 1 mL

\[ \frac{2 \times 1}{10} = 0.2\, \text{mL} \]
